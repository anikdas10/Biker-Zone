Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check for cracks, dents, or damage to the helmet shell. If the foam inside is compressed or deteriorating, it’s a sign of wear. Helmets typically have a lifespan of about 5 years, so check the manufacturing date. If it feels loose or the straps are worn, it’s time for a replacement.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect the tires for cracks or flat spots, and check if they hold air. Ensure the brakes work properly and aren’t stiff or rusted. Lubricate the chain and check for rust on any metal parts. Test the bike’s overall stability before taking it on the road.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet and visible clothing, and use lights at night. Obey traffic signals and ride in the same direction as traffic. Use hand signals when turning, and stay in designated bike lanes when possible. Be alert for cars, pedestrians, and other cyclists, maintaining a safe distance from them.
